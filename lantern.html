Lantern Festival

The Lantern Festival, also known as Yuan Xiao Festival, is celebrated on the 15th day of the Lunar New Year. It marks the end of the Chinese New Year celebrations and is known for its beautiful lantern displays, traditional dances, and delicious sweet rice dumplings called tangyuan.

What is the Lantern Festival?

The Lantern Festival is a traditional Chinese festival celebrated on the 15th day of the first lunar month, signaling the end of the Chinese New Year festivities. It is a time for families to come together, enjoy festive foods, and light colorful lanterns that symbolize hope and good fortune for the year ahead. Lantern displays, dragon dances, and the eating of tangyuan (sweet rice dumplings) are some of the most popular traditions associated with the festival.

The Lantern Festival is also a time to celebrate family unity and wish for a prosperous year. Lanterns of all shapes and sizes fill the night sky, creating a mesmerizing spectacle of light and color.
