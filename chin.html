What is Chinese New Year?

Chinese New Year is a celebration that marks the start of a new year according to the lunar calendar. It is based on the cycles of the moon, and it usually falls between January 21 and February 20. Each year is associated with one of the twelve animals in the Chinese zodiac, and the celebration lasts for 15 days, culminating in the Lantern Festival.

The festival is filled with various customs, including family gatherings, giving red envelopes (containing money) to children, and enjoying traditional foods. The color red is prominent throughout the celebrations, symbolizing good fortune and happiness.
