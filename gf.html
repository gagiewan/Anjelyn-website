Good Friday

Good Friday is a solemn observance in the Christian tradition that commemorates the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. It is observed on the Friday before Easter Sunday, during Holy Week, and serves as a reminder of the suffering, sacrifice, and death of Jesus for the salvation of humanity.

What is Good Friday?

Good Friday marks the day when Jesus Christ was crucified on the cross, as part of God's plan to redeem mankind from sin. According to Christian belief, Jesus' death serves as the ultimate sacrifice for humanity, offering salvation and eternal life to those who believe in Him.
