All Souls' Day

All Souls' Day, observed on **November 2nd**, is a day dedicated to honoring and praying for the souls of the departed. It follows **All Saints' Day** and is a significant religious observance in many Christian traditions, particularly in the **Philippines, Mexico, and other Catholic-majority countries**.

What is All Souls' Day?

All Souls' Day is a solemn day for remembering deceased loved ones and praying for their souls. Many families visit cemeteries, light candles, and offer **flowers, prayers, and Mass intentions** for their departed relatives.

Filipino families typically **clean and decorate graves, light candles, and gather for prayers and meals** in memory of the deceased. It is a time of reflection, honoring ancestors, and strengthening family ties.
