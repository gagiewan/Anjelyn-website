EDSA Revolution Day

EDSA Revolution Day, celebrated every February 25th, marks the historic People Power Revolution in the Philippines. It commemorates the peaceful overthrow of the dictatorship of President Ferdinand Marcos in 1986 through a four-day demonstration at Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) in Metro Manila.

What is EDSA Revolution Day?

The EDSA Revolution, also known as the People Power Revolution, was a nonviolent uprising that united millions of Filipinos from different walks of life. It was a pivotal event that restored democracy in the country, ending 21 years of authoritarian rule under Ferdinand Marcos. The revolution is celebrated as a triumph of unity, courage, and the power of the Filipino people to bring about change without violence.

On this day, Filipinos remember the sacrifices made by the people during the revolution and honor those who fought for democracy. EDSA Revolution Day serves as a reminder of the importance of free expression, unity, and the need to protect democratic institutions.
