What is the First Philippine Republic?

The First Philippine Republic was established on January 23, 1899, under the leadership of President Emilio Aguinaldo. It marked the Filipinos' aspiration for independence, a momentous event in Philippine history, as it aimed to form a democratic government free from colonial domination.

Although it was short-lived due to the Philippine-American War, the First Philippine Republic symbolized the Filipino people's determination for sovereignty and self-rule.
