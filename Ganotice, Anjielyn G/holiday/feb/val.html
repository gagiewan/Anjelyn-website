Valentine's Day

Valentine's Day is celebrated globally as a day of love and affection. It is observed on February 14th and is marked by the exchange of gifts, flowers, and heartfelt messages between loved ones.

What is Valentine's Day?

Valentine's Day, named after Saint Valentine, has evolved into a celebration of love and friendship. People celebrate by sending greeting cards, giving flowers (especially roses), sharing chocolates, and spending time with loved ones. It is a day to express affection, appreciation, and care for those who hold a special place in one's life.

From romantic gestures to acts of kindness, Valentine's Day is an opportunity to show love in all its forms. Whether through words, actions, or gifts, it brings people together in celebration of love.
