What is Constitution Day?

Constitution Day is observed on the day a country's constitution was adopted. This day serves to remind citizens of the values and principles that shape the nation's governance. It promotes awareness about the country's legal framework, the rights of individuals, and the responsibilities of the state. In many countries, Constitution Day is a public holiday marked by ceremonies, discussions, and educational activities to honor the constitution and its impact on the nation.

This day plays a vital role in strengthening the understanding of civic rights and responsibilities, ensuring that citizens continue to support the democratic framework established by the constitution.
