Ninoy Aquino Day

Ninoy Aquino Day is a special non-working holiday in the Philippines observed every **August 21**. It commemorates the assassination of **Senator Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr.**, a key figure in the fight against dictatorship and the restoration of democracy in the country.

Who was Ninoy Aquino?

Ninoy Aquino was a staunch critic of authoritarian rule in the Philippines. His unwavering stand against oppression led to his imprisonment and eventual exile. Upon his return to the country on August 21, 1983, he was tragically assassinated, an event that sparked widespread protests and led to the 1986 **People Power Revolution**.

His legacy continues to inspire Filipinos in their pursuit of justice, freedom, and good governance. Ninoy's famous words, **"The Filipino is worth dying for,"** remain a powerful symbol of patriotism and sacrifice.
