Day of Valor

The Day of Valor (Araw ng Kagitingan) is observed every April 9th in the Philippines, commemorating the heroism and sacrifices made by Filipino and American soldiers during World War II, particularly in the Battle of Bataan. It is a day to honor those who fought and died during the war and is a reminder of the resilience and bravery shown during one of the darkest chapters in Philippine history.

What is Araw ng Kagitingan?

Araw ng Kagitingan, or the Day of Valor, marks the anniversary of the fall of Bataan in 1942, when thousands of Filipino and American soldiers were forced to surrender to the invading Japanese forces. The day honors the courage and sacrifices of those who fought in the defense of the Philippines and the hardships they endured, including the infamous Bataan Death March.

On this day, ceremonies and programs are held across the country to remember the bravery of the soldiers. It serves as a reminder of the importance of freedom and the sacrifices made by the country's heroes to preserve it.
