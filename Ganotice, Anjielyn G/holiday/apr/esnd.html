Easter Sunday

Easter Sunday is the celebration of the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, a foundational event in Christian belief. It is observed on the first Sunday following the first full moon after the vernal equinox, and it marks the end of the Lenten season and the beginning of the Easter season. Easter Sunday symbolizes hope, renewal, and victory over death.

What is Easter Sunday?

Easter Sunday is the most important and joyous day in the Christian liturgical calendar. It commemorates the resurrection of Jesus Christ, who, according to Christian faith, was crucified, died, and was buried on Good Friday, but rose from the dead on Easter Sunday. The resurrection of Jesus signifies victory over sin and death, and it promises eternal life to all who believe in Him.

On Easter Sunday, Christians around the world celebrate with church services, feasts, and various festivities. The day is filled with joy and gratitude for the resurrection, and it serves as a reminder of God's power, love, and promise of eternal life. Many Christian denominations also hold the Easter Vigil on the night before Easter Sunday to mark the transition from darkness to light, symbolizing the resurrection of Christ.
