Bonifacio Day

Bonifacio Day is celebrated every **November 30th** in honor of **Andrés Bonifacio**, the Father of the **Philippine Revolution** and founder of the **Katipunan**, a revolutionary movement that fought for the country's independence from Spanish rule.

Who was Andrés Bonifacio?

Andrés Bonifacio was a Filipino nationalist and revolutionary leader. Unlike other leaders, he came from a working-class background and became a key figure in the fight for Philippine independence. His bravery, leadership, and vision inspired Filipinos to stand against colonial oppression.

On this day, Filipinos commemorate his legacy through **ceremonies, parades, and reenactments** of his heroic acts, remembering his contributions to the nation's freedom.
