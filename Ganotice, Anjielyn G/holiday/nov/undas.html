Undas / All Saints' Day

**Undas**, also known as **All Saints' Day**, is observed every **November 1st** in the Philippines. It is a day of remembrance and prayer for the departed loved ones, marked by visits to cemeteries, offering candles and flowers, and family gatherings.

What is All Saints' Day?

All Saints' Day is a Christian holiday that honors all known and unknown saints. In the Philippines, **Undas** is deeply rooted in tradition, where families gather to celebrate the lives of their ancestors, reflect on their legacy, and strengthen familial bonds.

Filipinos prepare special food, light candles, and offer prayers to show respect to their departed loved ones. The event is both a solemn and meaningful reunion of families, emphasizing faith and remembrance.
