New Year's Eve

New Year's Eve, observed on **December 31st**, marks the last day of the year and the night before **New Year's Day**. It is a time for reflection on the past year, celebration of new beginnings, and anticipation of the year ahead.

What Happens on New Year's Eve?

New Year's Eve is widely celebrated with **parties, fireworks, and countdown events**. People gather with family and friends to celebrate the end of one year and the beginning of another, often making resolutions for self-improvement in the upcoming year.

In the Philippines, **New Year's Eve** is celebrated with great enthusiasm, highlighted by fireworks, eating traditional food like **12 round fruits**, and the tradition of **Media Noche**, a midnight feast symbolizing abundance for the coming year.
